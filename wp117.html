Pufferfish: Nature's Inflatable Wonder

Pufferfish, known for their unique ability to inflate when threatened, are fascinating creatures of the ocean. Their distinct appearance and interesting behaviors make them a favorite among marine enthusiasts.

Found in tropical and subtropical waters, pufferfish are recognized for their round bodies and vibrant colors. When inflated, they become much larger, deterring potential predators. Some species contain tetrodotoxin, a potent toxin that makes them poisonous to eat, serving as an effective defense mechanism. Despite this, pufferfish are often sought after in the aquarium trade due to their unique charm and personality.
